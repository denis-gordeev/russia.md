Russia’s technology story is uneven but substantial. It includes mathematics, physics, aerospace, nuclear engineering, defense-linked R&D, internet companies, payment systems, and local digital ecosystems built under changing geopolitical constraints.

The pattern is not Silicon Valley style consumer startup mythology. A better frame is the interaction between strong scientific traditions, strategic state sectors, and selective private platform growth. That mix explains both technical strengths and structural bottlenecks.
