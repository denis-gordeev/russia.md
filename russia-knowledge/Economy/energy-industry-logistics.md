Russia’s economy is often summarized through hydrocarbons, but that is only the outer layer. Energy, metals, chemicals, agriculture, transport corridors, defense-linked production, and large domestic service sectors all matter.

The key structural challenge is diversification under conditions shaped by geography, state capacity, infrastructure, sanctions, and uneven regional development. Logistics is a particularly important lens because physical distance and export orientation shape almost every major sector.
